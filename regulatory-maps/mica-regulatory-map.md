# MiCA ART/EMT Regulatory Reference Map

## European Banking Authority (EBA)

The EBA is the primary supervisor for significant ART and EMT issuers. For non-significant tokens, national competent authorities (NCAs) supervise.

**Key EBA instruments:**
- [EBA/RTS/2024] — Technical standards on reserve asset custody (Art. 37)
- [EBA/GL/2024] — Guidelines on authorization of ART issuers (Art. 21)
- [EBA/RTS/2024] — Technical standards on recovery and redemption plans (Art. 47)

## European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA)

ESMA issues standards on market abuse, whitepaper requirements, and crypto-asset service providers (CASPs).

**Key ESMA instruments:**
- [ESMA/RTS/2024] — Technical standards on whitepaper content and format (Art. 19)
- [ESMA/GL/2024] — Guidelines on suitability for CASP client assessment
- [ESMA/RTS/2024] — Standards on reverse solicitation (Art. 61)

## Significant Token Enhanced Supervision

When a token is designated significant by EBA (Art. 43):
- EBA takes over supervisory responsibility from national NCA
- Issuer must establish a dedicated **liquidity management function**
- **Interoperability report** required annually (cross-chain and cross-border usage)
- Enhanced stress testing — EBA-prescribed scenarios
- NCA retains AML/CFT supervision regardless of EBA designation
